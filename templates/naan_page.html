About me

I am a leavened, oven-baked flatbread found in the cuisines of Southeast Asia, the Indian subcontinent and the Caribbean.

I am cooked in a tandoor, from which tandoori cooking gets its name. I am different from my friend roti, which is usually cooked on a flat iron griddle called a tava.

I am served hot and brushed with some ghee or butter brushed, along with my friends Dal Makhani and Paneer Butter Masala.

How am i made

  • Firstly, knead the dough for 10 minutes with ingredients for dough. Cover with a moist cloth and rest for 2 hours in warm place.

  • After 2 hours, knead the dough slightly to remove if any air present in the dough.

  • Pinch a ball sized dough. Gently roll to oval shape using rolling pin. Make sure you do not make too thin nor thick and also keep the size in mind. It should not be larger than your tawa size.

  • Grease with water over naan. Furthermore, slowly flip over and put it on hot tawa and slightly press.

  • Now after a minute, flip the tawa upside down and cook naan directly on flame till they turn golden brown.

  • Also brush with some butter mixed with coriander leaves.

  • Gently scrape the naan from bottom and remove. Finally, serve butter naan hot with your favourite curry like Paneer Butter masala.
